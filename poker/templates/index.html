How CFR Works

Each iteration traverses the full game tree across all 120 possible card deals, updating regret at every decision point.

Regret = how many chips we'd have gained by always taking action A. Actions with high regret get higher probability next iteration.

The average strategy over all iterations converges to Nash equilibrium — unexploitable by any opponent.

Exploitability measures distance from Nash. Watch it decrease as training progresses toward 0.
